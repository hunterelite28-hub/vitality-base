The Maintenance Tracker

The weight tracker and the macro tracker, connected — so the coach keeps you in your goal lane, warmly.

Build home: fuel-macros worktree (off clean main) · Status: review-approved, ready for the build plan

The one-liner

You weigh in every morning and log your food. The coach watches your real weekly trend, learns your true maintenance calories from your own results, and once a week suggests a small nudge to keep you in your lane. It only changes your numbers if you tap yes — and it's never pushy.

How the brain works

1 · How fast are you really moving?

Daily weight bounces. It reads the smooth 7-day line instead — invisible to you. You just see "up about half a pound this week."

2 · What can you really eat?

It works backwards to your real maintenance — your break-even. Shown softly, sharpening over time, never as gospel.

3 · Are you in your lane?

  • Climbing quick → ease back, "trim ~150."
  • Barely moving → add a bit, "+150."
  • In the zone → nailing it, change nothing.

The honesty rule. For the first ~2 weeks it stays quiet — "keep logging, first read in N days" — instead of inventing a number. It never stops; it just gets sharper.

Tone — the rule we don't cross locked

  • No red. No shame. No "you're failing." No scary down-arrows.
  • Out of range is said warm: "you're climbing a little quick, let's ease back together."
  • Feel coached by someone who likes you — motivated to step back in, never to cancel.
  • "Give me grace this week" is always one tap away.

What the coach does here

The coach doesn't remember every chat (too expensive). It knows you from your saved file, fresh each time. Its job:

  • The warm voice of the weekly nudge.
  • Answers "how am I tracking?" / "why did my target change?" from your numbers.
  • You say "rough week, I'm gonna eat more" → it takes that as a grace signal and skips judging.

The bad-tracker problem handled

The scale is the honest judge. The food log is just the dial.

  • Fib the log → the scale still shows you gaining too fast, and the scale is what we steer by.
  • We nudge relative to your own target, so a steady fib cancels out.
  • Maintenance shown soft, never a scary "crash."
  • If log and scale disagree: "your log's a little light next to your scale — normal, I'll trust the scale." No accusation.

How the data moves

weigh-in --> weights [ honest judge ] food logs --> calories [ the dial ] setup quiz --> goal lane + target | weekly: direction from the scale, size relative to your own target | nudge + reason --> pending | coach voices it warm, with your food story | tap Yes --> new target (re-split gym/rest) tap No --> nothing changes tap Grace --> week skipped, no judgement
